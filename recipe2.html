Smooth Dark Chocolate Mousse

This is a perfect dessert to whip up if you love chocolate!

Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 6 ounces of the best quality semi-sweet chocolate
  • 3 large eggs
  • 4 ripe bananas
  • 0.5 teaspoon of cream of tartar
  • 0.25 cup plus 2 tablespoons of granulated sugar
  • 0.5 cup of cold heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  1. Put the butter and chocolate in a heatproof bowl. Place the bowl on a double-boiler. Stir with a spatula until the chocolate is melted and the mixture is completely smooth.
  2. Allow the melted chocolate/butter mixture to cool for a few minutes. In the meantime, separate the 3 eggs’ whites and yolks into separate bowls.
  3. Once the few minutes have passed, whisk in the egg yolks one at a time until fully incorporated into the chocolate mixture.
  4. Using an electric hand mixture, beat the egg whites on medium speed until foamy. Add in the cream of tartar and beat on medium-high speed until soft peaks form. Begin to incorporate 0.25 cup of sugar until stiff peaks form (the egg whites should maintain a stiff point when the mixer is lifted from the bowl).
  5. Using a spatula, fold the egg white mixture into the chocolate mixture. Set aside.
  6. In a separate bowl, beat the heavy cream on medium-high speed with an electric mixer until the cream thickens. Add in the remaining 2 tablespoons of sugar and the vanilla. Continue beating until medium peaks (do not over mix).
  7. Fold the whipped cream into the chocolate mixture. Be sure to fully combine the two mixtures together, but do not over fold because it will remove air from the mousse.
  8. Scoop the mousse mixture into 6 individual glasses. Cover the glasses and chill them in the refrigerator for at least two hours.
  9. Once fully chilled, enjoy the mousse!
